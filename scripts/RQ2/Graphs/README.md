# RQ2 Graphs

This folder contains the figures generated for Research Question 2:

**RQ2: What are the most important motivators for SDG involvement in SME operations?**

The figures are aligned with the non-tautological modelling approach used in the RQ2 analysis. The variable `Sustainable_success_orientation` is not presented as a main motivator because it directly reflects whether the firm already includes sustainable goals in measuring business success.

## Figures

* `RQ2_01_top_nontautological_factors_frequency.png` – shows the most frequent active non-tautological motivational and institutional factors reported by SMEs.
* `RQ2_02_nontautological_binary_SDG_count_difference.png` – shows binary factors associated with a higher mean `SDG_count`, comparing firms that selected each factor with firms that did not.
* `RQ2_03_numeric_predictors_spearman_SDG_count.png` – shows numeric and scalar predictors ranked by their Spearman correlation with `SDG_count`.
* `RQ2_04_nontautological_negative_binomial_IRR.png` – presents the main non-tautological Negative Binomial model for `SDG_count`, using incidence rate ratios (IRR) with 95% confidence intervals.
* `RQ2_05_nontautological_logistic_OR_SDG_count_ge_2.png` – presents the logistic sensitivity model for `SDG_count >= 2`, using odds ratios (OR) with 95% confidence intervals.
* `RQ2_06_pressure_sources_negative_binomial_IRR.png` – presents the exploratory Negative Binomial model of specific pressure sources, using incidence rate ratios (IRR) with 95% confidence intervals.

The first three figures summarize descriptive and bivariate RQ2 results. The last three figures summarize the non-tautological multivariate modelling results. Together, these figures support the interpretation of the main RQ2 finding that broader sustainability-related pressure is the most consistent predictor of direct SDG involvement. The exploratory pressure-source model additionally suggests that bank-related pressure may be a particularly relevant individual pressure source.
